## The Resolution Explained

### The 2% Branch Activity Fund

The proposal would establish a Branch Activity Fund by ring-fencing a minimum of **2% of annual membership subscription income** exclusively for branch-led initiatives.

Potential uses include:

- Subsidised local CPD
- Student engagement
- Mentorship programmes
- Public awareness campaigns
- Local networking events
- Outreach and recruitment activity
- Educational workshops

### Democratic Oversight

Strategic priorities for the fund would be determined democratically by Branch Chairs through the existing Regional Branch Committee structure.

This supports:

- Local accountability
- Transparent governance
- Member influence over priorities
- Regional flexibility

## The Stepped Mandate

### If the resolution receives a 50% majority

A simple majority directs Council to implement the Branch Activity Fund through the By-Laws.

### If the resolution receives a 75% majority

A supermajority further directs Council to bring a Special Resolution to the 2027 AGM to protect the fund within the Articles of Association.

### Why this matters

Once protected within the Articles, the fund could not be removed without explicit member approval. This would give branches long-term stability and confidence when planning future activity.

### Member rights

Every Voting Member has a constitutional right to appoint a proxy to vote on their behalf.

By-Law 24 additionally permits proxies to be appointed in "any other form which is usual".

This may include authenticated electronic correspondence, signed physical letters, or other written forms. Final operational guidance should be confirmed once official AGM documentation is published.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Will this increase membership fees?

No. The proposal ring-fences a minimum proportion of existing subscription income.

### Who controls the funding?

### Why protect the fund in the Articles later?

Embedding protection within the Articles creates long-term certainty and prevents future removal without explicit member consent.

### Is the proposal constitutional?

Yes. The proposal is structured as an Ordinary Resolution operating as a binding direction to Council under Article 63.
