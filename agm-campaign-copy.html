Motion One: Help Branches Grow Again

Vote YES for the Branch Activity Fund Resolution.

2% fund

The Proposal

Create a sustainable funding framework by ring-fencing a minimum of 2% of annual membership subscription income for branch-led initiatives.

Local value

Could Support

  • Subsidised local CPD
  • Student engagement
  • Mentorship programmes
  • Public awareness campaigns
  • Networking and outreach
Democratic

Oversight

Strategic priorities would be determined democratically by Branch Chairs through the existing Regional Branch Committee structure.

Page Copy

Vote YES to Help Branches Grow Again.

Local branches are where many members experience the practical value of professional community: CPD, mentoring, networking, student support, outreach, and peer connection.

The Branch Activity Fund would help branches plan with confidence and reinvest member subscriptions into visible local value.

Motion Two: Member Consultation

Vote YES for proper member consultation on The Podiatrist.

Member voice

The Issue

This concerns the decision to move The Podiatrist to virtual-only without first consulting members.

Balanced

Positioning

The issue is not whether digital communication has value. The point is that major changes should involve proper consultation.

Member Email Copy

Draft email for members once ready to send.

Subject: AGM 2026: Your Vote Matters

Dear Member,

The 2026 Royal College of Podiatry AGM takes place on 6 June 2026.

Two member-led motions are expected to be considered at the AGM.

The first is the Branch Activity Fund Resolution. This proposal would ring-fence a minimum of 2% of annual membership subscription income for branch-led initiatives, helping to support local CPD, mentorship, student engagement, outreach, and networking.

The second relates to member consultation on the move to make The Podiatrist virtual-only. This motion is about ensuring members are properly consulted on significant changes to how they receive core College publications.

Both motions are about strengthening member voice, local value, and democratic accountability.

Formal AGM documentation is expected on 11 May 2026. At present, final clarification regarding proxy arrangements and timelines is still awaited.

We will share further updates as soon as official guidance is available.

In the meantime, please stay informed and be ready to participate.

Kind regards,
Royal College of Podiatry Glasgow Branch
